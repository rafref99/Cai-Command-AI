The Illusion of Chance

In the cold, logical heart of the machine lived randomness—or rather, its perfect imitation. Computer random numbers are not truly random; they are meticulously calculated sequences derived from complex algorithms, often seeded by a tiny initial value (the seed). They are deterministic, predictable if you know the starting point.

Yet, to the user, they appear magical, capable of predicting lottery winners or simulating natural chaos. The story of randomness in computing is thus a tale of elegant deception: a mathematical illusion so convincing that it passes for pure chance.
